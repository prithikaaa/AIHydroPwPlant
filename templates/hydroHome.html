HydroStorm

Abstract

HydroStorm is a website that harnesses machine learning (ML) models to revolutionize the design and planning of hydropower plants along rivers. By analyzing satellite images through advanced satellite image processing, HydroStorm identifies potential sites for hydropower plant construction by scrutinizing map patterns. The platform integrates ML models trained on image datasets depicting various dam statuses, including proposed, completed, and under construction, denoted by colored dots. Additionally, HydroStorm employs ML algorithms like logistic regression to forecast hydro power plant types such as Storage, Pumped Storage, and Run-of-River, using real datasets. Furthermore, it utilizes ML models like random forest, CT-Gan, multioutput regressor to predict dam height, reservoir capacity, and area, leveraging eight distinct inputs like water head height and wave energy, derived from real datasets of India. Data collection is facilitated through remote sensors. HydroStorm's backend is powered by Flask, while the frontend employs HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. By offering insights and tools tailored to civil engineers and architects, HydroStorm enhances project planning, sustainability, and resource optimization, fostering collaborative innovation in renewable energy infrastructure development.

How it Works

1. *Data Collection*: HydroStorm gathers data from various sources, including satellite images and real datasets, which depict dam statuses, water head height, wave energy, and more. Data collection is facilitated through remote sensors for accuracy and real-time information.
2. *Satellite Image Processing*: Advanced satellite image processing techniques are applied to analyze satellite images. These images are scrutinized to identify potential sites for hydropower plant construction by recognizing map patterns.
3. *Machine Learning Models*: HydroStorm integrates machine learning models trained on the collected datasets. These models include logistic regression for forecasting hydro power plant types and algorithms like random forest, CT-Gan, and multioutput regressor for predicting crucial factors such as dam height, reservoir capacity, and area.
4. *Backend and Frontend*: The backend of HydroStorm is powered by Flask, ensuring efficiency and reliability in processing and managing data. On the frontend, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript are utilized to create a user-friendly interface for civil engineers and architects.
5. *Insights and Tools*: HydroStorm offers tailored insights and tools based on the analyzed data. These insights aid in project planning, sustainability, and resource optimization, empowering users to make informed decisions.
